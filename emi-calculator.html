Make a larger down payment

Improve your CIBIL score first

How is EMI Calculated in India?

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is calculated using the formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n – 1), where P is the loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the number of months. This formula is used by all RBI-regulated banks and NBFCs in India including SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, and Axis Bank.

For example, a ₹10 lakh home loan at 8.5% for 20 years gives an EMI of ₹8,678. The same loan at 9.5% gives ₹9,321 — a difference of ₹643 per month or ₹1.54 lakh over the full tenure. This is why comparing interest rates before choosing a lender is critical.

What Affects Your EMI?

Three factors directly control your EMI amount: the loan amount (principal), the interest rate, and the loan tenure. A higher principal increases EMI. A higher rate increases EMI. A longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest paid. Most borrowers focus only on EMI amount — but total interest paid is equally important.

Your CIBIL score also indirectly affects EMI — borrowers with 750+ CIBIL score typically get 0.5–1% lower interest rates than those with 650 CIBIL. On a ₹30 lakh home loan over 20 years, that 1% difference saves approximately ₹3.8 lakh in total interest. Check your eligibility on our loan eligibility calculator before applying.

Home Loan vs Personal Loan — EMI Comparison

Home loans have lower interest rates (8–10%) and longer tenures (up to 30 years), making EMIs lower but the total repayment period longer. Personal loans have higher rates (10–24%) but shorter tenures (1–5 years), so EMIs are higher but you repay faster. Instant loan apps charge the most — up to 36% APR — making them suitable only for short-term emergencies. Use our loan comparison tool to see current rates across 50+ lenders.
