What Happens If You Can't Pay an Instant Loan After 30 Days?

Late fees, CIBIL damage, recovery calls, legal action — the full timeline explained with real numbers

📅 July 2026 ⏱ 6 min read ⚠️ Must Read 🇮🇳 India
⚠️ Important First
This article is for people who are struggling to repay — not to scare you, but to help you understand exactly what happens so you can take the right steps early. The earlier you act, the better the outcome.

You borrowed ₹20,000 from KreditBee or mPokket. The due date passed. You don't have the money. Your phone is ringing. What exactly happens next — day by day?

Most people panic and ignore calls, which makes things much worse. This article explains the exact timeline, the real cost of defaulting, and what you should do at each stage.

How Interest and Fees Pile Up — Real Calculation

Let's take a real example: ₹20,000 borrowed from KreditBee at 28% APR for 30 days.

Day What's Added Amount You Owe
Day 0 (borrow) Principal ₹20,000
Day 30 (due date) Interest for 30 days @ 28% APR ₹20,460
Day 31 (1 day late) + Late payment fee ₹500–₹1,000 ₹21,460
Day 60 (30 days late) + Penal interest @ 3% per month = ₹615 ₹22,075
Day 90 (60 days late) + Another month penal interest ₹22,737
Day 90+ (NPA) Reported to CIBIL as defaulter ₹22,737 + CIBIL damage

A ₹20,000 loan becomes ₹22,737 in 90 days — and that's before legal costs. The penal interest compounds every month you don't pay.

The Day-by-Day Recovery Timeline

Here is exactly what happens after you miss a payment:

D+1
Day 1 After Due Date — SMS and App Notifications
You receive automated SMS, email, and app push notifications reminding you of the overdue amount. No human contact yet. Late fee is added automatically to your account.
D+3
Day 3 — First Phone Call
A customer service agent calls from the app's collections team. Polite at this stage. They ask when you can pay and offer a small extension in some cases. Pick up this call — it's your best chance to negotiate.
D+7
Day 7 — Multiple Daily Calls
Calls increase to 2–4 times per day. SMS frequency increases. Some apps send WhatsApp messages. Tone becomes more urgent but still polite. They may offer a settlement or partial payment plan.
D+15
Day 15 — Emergency Contact Calls Begin
Some apps (especially smaller ones) start calling the emergency contacts you provided during loan application — family members, friends. This is legal if you gave consent in the loan agreement (most agreements include this). Highly embarrassing and stressful.
D+30
Day 30 — Transferred to Collections Agency
The app sells or transfers your loan to a third-party recovery/collections agency. New people start calling from different numbers. Tone becomes more aggressive. They may visit your address.
D+60
Day 60 — CIBIL Report Flagged
Your account is marked as "SMA-1" (Special Mention Account) in CIBIL. Your credit score starts dropping. Future loan applications begin getting rejected even if you clear this later.
D+90
Day 90 — NPA Declaration + Major CIBIL Damage
Account declared NPA (Non-Performing Asset). CIBIL score drops 50–100 points. Marked as "defaulter" in credit report. This stays on your record for 7 years. Getting any loan — home, car, personal — becomes very difficult.
D+180
Day 180 — Legal Notice
For amounts above ₹50,000, lenders may file a case in civil court or send a legal notice. For smaller amounts, they typically write it off but the CIBIL damage remains. Legal costs add to your outstanding amount.

What Recovery Agents Can and Cannot Do

RBI has strict rules about loan recovery. Many borrowers don't know their rights. Here's what is legal and illegal:

✅ Legal — They CAN Do This
  • Call you between 8 AM and 7 PM only
  • Send SMS and email reminders
  • Call emergency contacts you listed
  • Visit your registered address
  • Send legal notice
  • File civil suit in court
  • Report to CIBIL
❌ Illegal — They CANNOT Do This
  • Call before 8 AM or after 7 PM
  • Use abusive or threatening language
  • Contact your employer without consent
  • Post on your social media
  • Share your photo or personal data publicly
  • Threaten physical harm
  • Impersonate police or government officials
🚨 If Recovery Agents Harass You
File a complaint immediately at:
• RBI Ombudsman: cms.rbi.org.in (free, online)
• Your state's cyber crime cell if they threaten or share your data
• NBFC's grievance officer (details on their app/website)

Harassment by recovery agents is a criminal offence under IPC. Many borrowers have successfully sued aggressive recovery agents.

CIBIL Score Impact — How Bad Is It?

Days Overdue CIBIL Status Score Impact Effect
1–30 days SMA-0 -10 to -20 points Minor — most banks overlook once
31–60 days SMA-1 -30 to -50 points Visible — some lenders reject
61–90 days SMA-2 -50 to -80 points Serious — most lenders reject
90+ days NPA / Defaulter -100 points or more Severe — home/car loan impossible for years

The worst part — even after you repay the full amount, the "settled" or "written off" tag stays on your CIBIL for 7 years. Banks can see this history and may still reject you even if your current score is good.

What You Should Do Right Now

If you have already missed a payment or know you will miss one, here are the steps to take — in order:

Step 1 — Don't Ignore, Don't Panic
Ignoring calls makes everything worse. The interest keeps growing. The agency gets more aggressive. Pick up the call and talk to them — you have more negotiating power than you think.
Step 2 — Request an Extension or Restructuring
Most apps will give a 7–15 day extension if you ask before the due date. After the due date, ask for an EMI restructuring — pay in 2–3 instalments instead of one lump sum. Many apps agree to this to avoid NPA classification.
Step 3 — Negotiate a Settlement
If you genuinely cannot pay the full amount, offer a one-time settlement (OTS). For example, if you owe ₹22,000, offer ₹18,000 as full and final settlement. Many NBFCs accept 70–80% to close the case. Get the settlement in writing before paying.
Step 4 — Borrow from Family to Clear It
If possible, borrow from family or friends to clear the app loan immediately. Even at 0% informal interest, clearing a 28–36% loan early saves a lot. Explain the situation honestly — the CIBIL damage affects your future far more.
Step 5 — If Harassment Happens, Complain
If agents threaten you, use abusive language, or contact your family in an inappropriate way — file a complaint with RBI Ombudsman immediately at cms.rbi.org.in. This is free and the NBFC is legally required to respond within 30 days.

One More Loan to Pay Another — Never Do This

The most common mistake people make: taking a second instant loan from another app to repay the first one. This creates a debt trap that is very hard to escape.

🚫 The Debt Trap Cycle
Borrow ₹20,000 from App A → Can't pay → Borrow ₹22,000 from App B to pay App A → Can't pay App B → Borrow ₹25,000 from App C → Within 3 months you owe ₹25,000 on a ₹20,000 original loan and have 3 apps calling you.

This is exactly how people end up in ₹1–2 lakh debt from a ₹20,000 loan.

How to Avoid This Situation in Future

  • Never borrow more than 10% of your monthly income from an instant app
  • Keep an emergency fund of 1 month's salary in a savings account
  • Use instant apps only for genuine emergencies — not for shopping or lifestyle expenses
  • Read the late fee clause before borrowing — it's always in the loan agreement
  • Set a reminder 5 days before the due date so you never miss it accidentally
  • Build your CIBIL score — a 750+ score gets you bank loans at 10% instead of app loans at 30%
