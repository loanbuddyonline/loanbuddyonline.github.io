How to Get a Personal Loan with Low CIBIL Score (600–680) in 2026

📅 June 2026⏱️ 6 min read💰 Personal Loan
Quick answer: You can get a personal loan with CIBIL 600–680, but not from traditional banks. NBFCs and instant loan apps (KreditBee, MoneyView, Stashfin) approve at 20–35% rate. Your best move: borrow, repay perfectly for 12 months, then refinance at a lower rate with a better score.

Why banks reject below 700 CIBIL

Most public sector banks (SBI, Bank of Baroda, Canara) and large private banks (HDFC, ICICI) require a minimum CIBIL of 700–720 for personal loans. Below this threshold, their risk models flag you as likely to default — even if you have a stable job and consistent income.

This is not personal. It is a statistical cutoff. Banks process millions of applications and use automated scoring. A 620 CIBIL triggers auto-rejection before a human reviews your file.

5 things that improve your chances despite low CIBIL

1. Show stable salary credits in bank account

If your salary has been crediting consistently to the same bank account for 6+ months, many NBFCs will use bank statement analysis (not just CIBIL) to approve your loan. Keep all income in one account — avoid cash withdrawals that make income look irregular.

2. Apply for a lower amount than you need

If you need ₹1.5L, apply for ₹1L. Smaller loans have higher approval rates at lower CIBIL. Get approved, repay perfectly, then apply for a top-up or a new loan after 6 months — by which time your CIBIL will have improved.

3. Add a co-applicant with good CIBIL

If a family member (spouse, parent, sibling) has a CIBIL score of 700+, adding them as co-applicant can unlock bank-level rates for you. The bank evaluates the combined credit profile. This works especially well for home loans.

4. Provide collateral (secured personal loan)

A gold loan, loan against FD, or loan against property requires no minimum CIBIL — the asset is the security. Gold loans are available at 7–11% rate regardless of credit score, since the bank holds physical gold. If you have any asset, this is always cheaper than an unsecured personal loan at 30%.

5. Clear existing defaults before applying

If you have any overdue EMIs or credit card dues, clear them before applying anywhere. Even one active default (DPD 30+) will cause rejection at most NBFCs. Clearing dues and waiting 30 days for CIBIL to update can improve approval rates significantly.

The rate trap — and how to avoid it

Some lenders use "risk-based pricing" — they approve your loan at the higher end of their rate range. On a ₹1 lakh loan at 36% for 24 months, you pay ₹26,000 in interest. On the same loan at 18%, you pay ₹12,000. The difference is ₹14,000 — on a ₹1 lakh loan.

Before accepting any offer: Calculate the total interest payable using our EMI Calculator. If the total interest exceeds 25% of the loan amount for a 12-month loan, the rate is above 45% APR — very high. Try another lender first.

The 12-month recovery plan

If you need a loan urgently and take one at 25–30% rate, here is the plan to get out of the high-rate trap:

  1. Take the smallest loan you need (₹50,000 – ₹1L) from an NBFC
  2. Set auto-debit and never miss a payment for 12 months
  3. In parallel, reduce credit card utilization below 30%
  4. After 12 months, CIBIL should be 680–720+
  5. Apply to HDFC or ICICI for a personal loan at 11–14% rate
  6. Use the new loan to close the expensive NBFC loan (balance transfer)

This two-step approach — expensive loan now, cheap loan later — is the fastest legitimate path from high-rate to low-rate credit.
