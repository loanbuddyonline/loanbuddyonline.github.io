📚 What is CIBIL Score?

CIBIL (Credit Information Bureau India Limited) score is a 3-digit number from 300 to 900 that represents your creditworthiness. It is calculated by TransUnion CIBIL based on your loan and credit card repayment history. Higher score = lower risk for banks = better loan terms for you.

🏛️ Who calculates it?
TransUnion CIBIL — India's oldest credit bureau. Also: Experian, Equifax, CRIF Highmark. Banks report monthly to all 4 bureaus.
🔄 How often updated?
Banks report to CIBIL every 30–45 days. Your score updates monthly. Expect a 30-60 day lag before repayments reflect in score.
🏦 Why banks care?
90% of loan decisions start with a CIBIL check. Score <700 = higher rate or rejection. Score 750+ = best rates, faster approval.

⚙️ 5 Factors That Affect Your CIBIL Score

💳 Payment History
35%
Single biggest factor. One missed EMI or bounced payment can drop score by 50–100 points. Always pay on or before due date. Set auto-debit.
💰 Credit Utilization
30%
How much of your credit card limit you use. Keep usage below 30% at all times. If limit is ₹1 lakh, never let outstanding exceed ₹30,000. Pay in full monthly.
📅 Credit Age
15%
Average age of all your credit accounts. Older is better. Never close your oldest credit card — it reduces average credit age and hurts your score.
🔀 Credit Mix
10%
Mix of secured (home, car) and unsecured (personal loan, credit card) credit. Having both improves score. Pure credit card users score lower than those with a loan mix.
🔍 New Enquiries
10%
Each time a bank pulls your CIBIL (hard inquiry), score drops 5–15 points. Applying to 5 banks at once = 5 hard inquiries. Apply to 1 bank at a time — wait for result before trying another.

📈 How to Improve Your CIBIL Score

Proven steps — sorted by impact. Start with the top ones first.

ASAP
✅ Pay all overdue EMIs immediately
If you have missed EMIs or defaults, clear them right now. Accounts in default (DPD > 90 days) heavily drag the score. Even partial payment helps — settle the outstanding and request a No-Objection Certificate (NOC) from the lender.
Week 1
⚡ Set auto-debit for all EMIs and credit cards
One missed payment = 50–100 point drop. Set standing instructions from your salary account for all loan EMIs and minimum credit card payments. Ensure enough balance 2 days before due date.
Month 1
💳 Reduce credit card utilization below 30%
If your card limit is ₹50,000 and outstanding is ₹40,000 (80% utilization), pay it down to below ₹15,000. Alternatively, request a credit limit increase from your bank — same spending, lower utilization ratio. Works within 30 days.
Month 1–2
🔍 Dispute errors in your CIBIL report
30% of CIBIL reports have at least one error. Get your free report and check: wrong loan accounts, settled loans showing as active, incorrect personal details. Dispute errors at cibil.com → Dispute Resolution. Corrected reports improve score in 30–45 days.
Month 2–3
🔒 Get a secured credit card (if you have no cards)
Open a Fixed Deposit (₹5,000–₹10,000) and get a secured credit card against it from SBI, HDFC, or Axis. Use it for small purchases (fuel, groceries) and pay full balance monthly. Builds credit history with zero risk of overspending.
Month 3+
🚫 Stop applying to multiple lenders simultaneously
Each application triggers a hard inquiry (-5 to -15 points). Apply to one lender, wait 2–3 weeks. If rejected, wait 3 months before next application. Use eligibility calculators (like ours) to pre-check without affecting CIBIL.
Month 6+
📊 Don't close old credit accounts
Closing old credit cards reduces average credit age and total available credit limit — both hurt the score. Keep old cards open with occasional small purchases. If there's an annual fee, negotiate a waiver or downgrade to a free variant.

⚡ CIBIL Myths vs Facts

❌ MYTH: Checking score hurts it
Checking your own score is a soft inquiry — has zero effect on CIBIL. Only hard inquiries (bank loan applications) reduce score.
✅ FACT: Check it monthly for free
Check using OneScore, Paisabazaar, or CRED as many times as you want — free and harmless. Monitoring helps you catch errors early.
❌ MYTH: High income = high CIBIL
CIBIL is entirely based on repayment history — not salary. A ₹15K/month gig worker who pays EMIs on time can have 780 CIBIL.
✅ FACT: Behavior matters, not income
Timely payment of even small loans and credit cards — ₹1,000 minimum credit card bill — builds strong CIBIL regardless of salary.
❌ MYTH: Settling a loan is good for CIBIL
"Settled" status (paying less than full amount) stays in CIBIL for 7 years and signals default. Banks often reject settled accounts.
✅ FACT: Pay the full amount and get NOC
Always pay the complete outstanding and get a No-Objection Certificate. Only full closure shows "Closed" status — settlement shows "Settled" (bad).
❌ MYTH: No loans = good CIBIL
"NH" or "No History" on CIBIL means no credit record — not good credit. Banks often reject or charge higher rates for no-history applicants.
✅ FACT: Start a credit record ASAP
Get a secured credit card or small personal loan and repay perfectly. 6 months of credit history is enough to get a CIBIL score of 700+.
